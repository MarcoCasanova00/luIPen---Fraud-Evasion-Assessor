Documentation & Methodology

How Fraud Detection Works

Modern fraud detection systems use Machine Learning (ML) models, such as Random Forests or Neural Networks, to analyze thousands of data points in real-time. They look for anomalies—patterns that deviate from a user's normal behavior.

This tool simulates a Random Forest Classifier trained on over 1,000,000 credit card transactions. It evaluates the probability that a specific transaction configuration will trigger a fraud alert.

Key Risk Factors

  • Geolocation Anomalies: High distances between the user's home (billing address) and the transaction IP are strong indicators of account takeover.
  • Velocity Checks: "Teleportation" (impossible travel) between the last transaction location and the current one is a critical red flag.
  • Spending Patterns: Transactions significantly larger than the user's median purchase history (High Ratio) are scrutinized.
  • Authentication Strength: Chip & PIN (Card Present) is the gold standard. Online (CNP) transactions lack this physical proof, increasing risk.

Understanding the Score

Bypass Success Score = 1.0 - Fraud Probability
